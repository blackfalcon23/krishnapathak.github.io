From School to Graduate School

Illuminating the Path of an Electrical Engineer

Welcome to the 'Little About My Self' section, where I invite you to explore the transformative journey that shaped me into an Electrical Engineer. From the foundational years of school to the advanced studies of graduate school, each step of my educational path has been a milestone in my pursuit of excellence. Here, you will witness the evolution of my skills, knowledge, and passion for electrical engineering.

School of Induction

I am a proud graduate of R.S.M. Poonawala Sarvajanik Experimental School, where my passion for electrical engineering was ignited. The comprehensive curriculum and hands-on projects provided a solid foundation in designing circuits, troubleshooting complex systems, and exploring cutting-edge technologies. Through active involvement in robotics clubs and science fairs, I further nurtured my innovation and problem-solving skills. These transformative experiences at the school fueled my drive for continuous learning and laid the groundwork for my exciting journey as an Electrical Engineer

Electromagnetic Evolution

Sarvajanik College of Engineering & Technology shaped my path as an Electrical Engineer. With a comprehensive curriculum and hands-on projects, I gained a deep understanding of power systems, electronics, and control systems. Engaging in research initiatives and active involvement in student organizations further nurtured my passion for innovation and problem-solving. The college provided the perfect platform for me to develop technical expertise, creativity, and a drive for continuous learning

Induction Illuminated

As a graduate of The University of Windsor's esteemed Masters in Electrical and Computer Engineering program, I immersed myself in the world of power systems, signal processing, and renewable energy. shaping my future as an Electrical Engineer. Through advanced coursework, hands-on projects, and research initiatives, I gained expertise in these fields, exploring cutting-edge techniques and collaborating with accomplished faculty. Industry-oriented projects and internships provided practical experience, while my passion for robotics fueled my active participation in competitions and collaborative projects. The University of Windsor instilled in me a commitment to lifelong learning, innovative thinking, and making a positive impact in the field of Electrical Engineering
